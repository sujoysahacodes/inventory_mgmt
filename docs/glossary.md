# Glossary

## Core Terms

### **ABC Analysis**
Inventory categorization method that divides items into three categories:
- **A Items**: High-value items requiring tight control
- **B Items**: Moderate-value items with moderate control
- **C Items**: Low-value items with simple controls

### **Available to Promise (ATP)**
The portion of inventory that is uncommitted and available for new customer orders.

### **Backorder**
An order for goods that are temporarily out of stock but will be fulfilled when inventory becomes available.

### **Carrying Cost**
The total cost of holding inventory, including storage, insurance, taxes, and opportunity costs.

### **Cycle Stock**
The portion of inventory needed to satisfy average demand between inventory replenishments.

### **Dead Stock**
Inventory items that have no demand and cannot be sold through normal channels.

### **Economic Order Quantity (EOQ)**
The optimal order quantity that minimizes total inventory costs (ordering + carrying costs).

### **First In, First Out (FIFO)**
Inventory valuation method where the first items received are the first ones issued or sold.

### **Just-in-Time (JIT)**
Inventory strategy that aligns raw material orders with production schedules to reduce waste.

### **Lead Time**
The time elapsed between placing an order and receiving the goods.

### **Last In, First Out (LIFO)**
Inventory valuation method where the most recently received items are the first ones issued or sold.

### **Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ)**
The smallest amount of inventory that a supplier is willing to sell.

### **Perpetual Inventory**
A system that continuously tracks inventory levels in real-time.

### **Reorder Point**
The inventory level at which a new order should be placed to avoid stockouts.

### **Safety Stock**
Extra inventory held to guard against uncertainties in demand and supply.

### **Stockout**
A situation where inventory is depleted and unable to fulfill customer demand.

### **Turnover Ratio**
A measure of how many times inventory is sold and replaced over a period.

## Technology Terms

### **RFID (Radio Frequency Identification)**
Technology that uses radio waves to identify and track inventory items automatically.

### **WMS (Warehouse Management System)**
Software application that supports day-to-day warehouse operations.

### **ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)**
Integrated management of main business processes through software and technology.

### **SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)**
A unique identifier for each distinct product and service that can be purchased.
